Vegetarian Phở Chay

Vietnamese noodle soup with charred aromatics, whole spices & steamed tofu
DeviceTM6
Prep15 min
Cook35 min~65 min
Total50 min~80 min
Servings48

Ingredients

  • Broth — Aromatics & Spices
  • 3 whole star anise (~3 g)
  • 5 whole cloves (~1 g)
  • 1 cinnamon stick (~8 g)
  • 3 g coriander seeds
  • 2 green cardamom pods (~1 g)
  • 200 g yellow onion, quartered
  • 30 g fresh ginger, sliced (skin on)
  • 15 g neutral oil
  • Broth — Liquid & Umami
  • 1000 g vegetable stock, low-sodium
  • 10 g dried shiitake (~3–4 pieces)
  • 120 g carrot, halved lengthwise
  • 100 g daikon radish, peeled, chunks (optional)
  • 25 g soy sauce (or tamari)
  • 5 g sugar (rock sugar preferred)
  • Varoma — Steamed Toppings
  • 200 g extra-firm tofu, sliced 1 cm
  • 200 g baby bok choy, halved
  • Noodles & Garnish (outside TM)
  • 200 g dried flat rice noodles (bánh phở)
  • 100 g bean sprouts
  • 15 g Thai basil
  • 15 g cilantro
  • 10 g fresh mint
  • 2 limes, quartered
  • 1–2 jalapeño or bird’s eye chili, sliced
  • 3–4 green onions, sliced
  • Hoisin & sriracha, to serve
  • Finishing
  • 5–6 g salt (reduce if stock is salted)
  • 15 ml lime juice

Ingredients (doubled — 2 batches of broth)

Each bowl makes one full batch of broth. Varoma toppings, noodles, and garnish are prepared at double quantity. Only the first batch uses Varoma for steaming.

  • Broth (total for both batches)
  • 6 star anise (3 per batch)
  • 10 cloves (5 per batch)
  • 2 cinnamon sticks (1 per batch)
  • 6 g coriander seeds (3 g per batch)
  • 4 cardamom pods (2 per batch)
  • 400 g yellow onion (200 g per batch)
  • 60 g fresh ginger (30 g per batch)
  • 30 g neutral oil (15 g per batch)
  • 2000 g vegetable stock (1000 g per batch)
  • 20 g dried shiitake (10 g per batch)
  • 240 g carrot (120 g per batch)
  • 200 g daikon (100 g per batch, optional)
  • 50 g soy sauce (25 g per batch)
  • 10 g sugar (5 g per batch)
  • 10–12 g salt (5–6 g per batch)
  • 30 ml lime juice (15 ml per batch)
  • Varoma & Toppings (total)
  • 400 g extra-firm tofu, sliced 1 cm
  • 400 g baby bok choy, halved
  • Noodles & Garnish (total)
  • 400 g dried flat rice noodles
  • 200 g bean sprouts
  • 30 g Thai basil
  • 30 g cilantro
  • 20 g mint
  • 4 limes, quartered
  • 2–3 chilis, sliced
  • 6 green onions, sliced
  • Hoisin & sriracha, to serve

Preparation

  1. Place star anise, cloves, cinnamon, coriander seeds, and cardamom into mixing bowl. Toast 3 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  2. Add onion quarters, ginger, and oil. Sauté 5 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  3. Add vegetable stock, dried shiitake, carrot halves, daikon (if using), soy sauce, and sugar. Stir to combine.
  4. Place tofu in Varoma dish, bok choy on Varoma tray. Set Varoma on lid. Remove measuring cup (steam must escape). Cook 25 min / Varoma / speed 1.
  5. While broth simmers: soak rice noodles in boiling water 15–20 min until pliable. Drain and rinse with cold water. Prepare garnish platter.
  6. Remove Varoma. Transfer tofu and bok choy to a plate, cover to keep warm.
  7. Insert simmering basket to strain broth. Press on solids, discard. Remove basket.
  8. Add salt (5–6 g) and lime juice (15 ml). Mix 10 sec / speed 2. Taste — should be savory, lightly sweet, and bright.
  9. Assemble: Divide noodles among 4 bowls. Top with tofu, bok choy, bean sprouts, green onion. Ladle hot broth over. Serve with basil, cilantro, mint, lime, chili, hoisin, sriracha.

Preparation (2 Bowls)

Strategy: Bowl 1 makes broth + Varoma steaming in one shot. During its 25-min cook, prep all garnishes and load Bowl 2 with pre-measured broth ingredients. Bowl 2 runs without Varoma (toppings already done) — use the steam vent time for extra tofu prep if needed.

Time saved vs two separate cooks: ~20 min (shared Varoma session, all garnish done during B1 simmer, immediate B2 start).

Bowl 2 (broth only) · ~35 min
  1. Bowl 1 Place half the spices (3 star anise, 5 cloves, 1 cinnamon, 3 g coriander, 2 cardamom) into mixing bowl. Toast 3 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  2. Bowl 1 Add 200 g onion, 30 g ginger, 15 g oil. Sauté 5 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  3. Bowl 1 Add 1000 g stock, 10 g shiitake, 120 g carrot, 100 g daikon, 25 g soy sauce, 5 g sugar.
  4. Bowl 1 Load Varoma: all tofu (400 g) in dish, all bok choy (400 g) on tray. Set on lid. Remove measuring cup. Cook 25 min / Varoma / speed 1.
  5. ⏲ During B1 Varoma Soak all 400 g rice noodles in boiling water. Pre-measure Bowl 2 broth ingredients (spices, 200 g onion, 30 g ginger, 15 g oil, 1000 g stock, etc.) into separate containers. Prepare full garnish platter (sprouts, herbs, limes, chili, onion).
  6. Bowl 1 Remove Varoma. Transfer all steamed tofu and bok choy to a covered plate — this serves all 8 portions.
  7. Bowl 1 Insert simmering basket to strain broth. Press solids, discard. Remove basket. Add 5–6 g salt + 15 ml lime juice. Mix 10 sec / speed 2. Pour into a large pot, cover to keep hot. Remove Bowl 1.
  8. ⏲ Swap Place Bowl 2 on base. Everything is pre-measured — zero delay.
  9. Bowl 2 Add remaining spices. Toast 3 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  10. Bowl 2 Add 200 g onion, 30 g ginger, 15 g oil. Sauté 5 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  11. Bowl 2 Add 1000 g stock, 10 g shiitake, 120 g carrot, 100 g daikon, 25 g soy sauce, 5 g sugar. Cook 25 min / 100°C / speed 1 (no Varoma needed — toppings already steamed). Measuring cup on lid.
  12. Bowl 2 Strain with simmering basket. Season with salt + lime juice. Mix 10 sec / speed 2. Combine with first batch.
  13. Assemble 8 bowls: Divide drained noodles. Top with steamed tofu, bok choy, sprouts, green onion. Ladle piping hot broth over. Serve with herbs, lime, chili, hoisin, sriracha.

Tips & Tricks

  • Charring hack: For authentic char, halve onion and ginger and char cut-side down in a dry skillet 3–4 min before adding to TM at step 2.
  • Mushroom powder boost: Add 5 g mushroom bouillon (Trader Joe’s umami seasoning works) with the stock for deeper umami.
  • Noodle texture: Soak in just-boiled water, don’t boil. This gives chewier, more authentic texture.
  • Broth freezes well: Up to 3 months. Great for instant weeknight phở.
  • 2-bowl strategy: ~80 min for 8 servings. Bowl 1's Varoma session handles ALL tofu and bok choy for both batches. Bowl 2 only needs to simmer broth — no Varoma, faster turnaround.
