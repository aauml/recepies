Vegetarian Phở Chay

Vietnamese noodle soup with charred aromatics, whole spices & steamed tofu
Device: Thermomix® TM6 Prep: 15 min Cook: 35 min Total: 50 min Servings: 4

Ingredients

Broth — Aromatics & Spices

  • 3 whole star anise (~3 g)
  • 5 whole cloves (~1 g)
  • 1 cinnamon stick (~8 g)
  • 3 g coriander seeds (~1 tsp)
  • 2 green cardamom pods (~1 g)
  • 200 g yellow onion (1 large), quartered
  • 30 g fresh ginger, sliced into coins (skin on)
  • 15 g neutral oil (sunflower or avocado)

Broth — Liquid & Umami

  • 1000 g vegetable stock, low-sodium
  • 10 g dried shiitake mushrooms (~3–4 pieces)
  • 120 g carrot (1 medium), halved lengthwise
  • 100 g daikon radish, peeled, in chunks (optional — adds sweetness)
  • 25 g soy sauce (or tamari for GF)
  • 5 g sugar (rock sugar preferred, granulated fine)

Varoma — Steamed Toppings

  • 200 g extra-firm tofu, sliced 1 cm thick
  • 200 g baby bok choy (2–3 heads), halved lengthwise

Noodles & Garnish (prepared outside TM)

  • 200 g dried flat rice noodles (bánh phở, ~5 mm width)
  • 100 g bean sprouts
  • 15 g fresh Thai basil (½ bunch)
  • 15 g fresh cilantro (½ bunch)
  • 10 g fresh mint (~6–8 leaves)
  • 2 limes, quartered (~60 ml juice)
  • 1–2 jalapeño or bird's eye chili, thinly sliced
  • 3–4 green onions, thinly sliced
  • Hoisin sauce, to serve
  • Sriracha, to serve

Finishing

  • 5–6 g salt (to taste — reduce if stock is salted)
  • 15 ml lime juice (½ lime, squeezed into broth)

Steps

  1. Place star anise, cloves, cinnamon stick, coriander seeds, and cardamom pods into mixing bowl. Toast dry spices 3 min / 120°C / speed 1. Fragrant aroma will fill the kitchen — this is the backbone of phở.
  2. Add onion quarters, ginger coins, and neutral oil to bowl. Sauté 5 min / 120°C / speed 1. The onion and ginger will soften and brown lightly, building the charred aromatic base.
  3. Add vegetable stock, dried shiitake mushrooms, carrot halves, daikon (if using), soy sauce, and sugar to bowl. Stir to combine.
  4. Place tofu slices into Varoma dish. Arrange bok choy halves on Varoma tray. Set Varoma assembly on top of mixing bowl lid. Remove measuring cup (steam must escape). Cook 25 min / Varoma / speed 1.
  5. While broth simmers: soak rice noodles in a large bowl of boiling water (from kettle) for 15–20 min or until pliable but still chewy. Drain and rinse with cold water. Prepare all garnishes on a serving platter.
  6. Remove Varoma. Transfer steamed tofu and bok choy to a plate and cover to keep warm.
  7. Insert simmering basket into mixing bowl to strain broth. Press gently on solids with spatula, then discard solids. Remove simmering basket. You should have a clear, amber, intensely fragrant broth.
  8. Season: add salt (5–6 g) and lime juice (15 ml) to broth. Mix 10 sec / speed 2. Taste and adjust — the broth should be savory, lightly sweet, and bright.
  9. Assemble bowls: Divide noodles among 4 large bowls. Top with steamed tofu, bok choy, bean sprouts, and sliced green onion. Ladle hot broth over noodles. Serve immediately with Thai basil, cilantro, mint, lime wedges, sliced chili, hoisin, and sriracha alongside.

Tips & Tricks

  • Mushroom powder boost: For deeper umami, add 5 g mushroom bouillon powder (or MSG) with the stock in step 3. Trader Joe's carries mushroom umami seasoning that works perfectly.
  • Charring hack: If you want a more authentic char on the onion and ginger, halve them and char cut-side down in a dry skillet for 3–4 min before adding to the TM at step 2. This adds a smoky depth the TM sauté can't fully replicate.
  • Noodle texture: Don't boil the rice noodles — soaking in just-boiled water gives a chewier, more authentic texture. If they feel too firm, dunk briefly in boiling water for 10–15 sec.
  • Leftover broth: This broth freezes beautifully for up to 3 months. Double the broth ingredients (skip the Varoma step) and freeze in 500 ml portions for instant phở on weeknights.
