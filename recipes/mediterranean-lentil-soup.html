Mediterranean Lentil Soup

Two-bowl double batch · Preserved lemon · Herby oil finish

DeviceThermomix® TM6
Prep15 min
Active cook~65 min
Servings8 (double batch)
Workflow2-bowl parallel

Ingredients

Per Bowl — Make Twice (Bowl 1 + Bowl 2)

  • 30 ml extra virgin olive oil (for sautéing)
  • 200 g yellow onion (1 large), quartered
  • 180 g carrots (~3 medium), roughly chunked
  • 22 g garlic (6 cloves)
  • 18 g preserved lemon peel, finely chopped by hand
  • 1 tsp Aleppo pepper (or ½ tsp mild chilli flakes)
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 700 ml vegetable broth
  • 150 ml water
  • 200 g green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 2 bay leaves + 8 fresh thyme sprigs (simmering basket)
  • 12 g nutritional yeast (~2–3 tbsp)
  • 7 g kosher salt (~1¼ tsp), plus more to taste
  • 15 ml lemon juice (½ lemon), added at the end

Herby Oil — One Batch Serves All 8

  • 30 ml good-quality extra virgin olive oil
  • 16 g fresh basil, dill, and/or flat-leaf parsley
  • 1 lemon, zested
  • 2 pinches flaky sea salt

Yogurt Sauce — Optional, Serves All 8

  • 240 g plain vegan yogurt (Culina or Cocojune recommended)
  • 20 ml lemon juice
  • 1 pinch each salt and black pepper

Steps

1
Bowl 2
Add 180 g chunked carrots and 22 g garlic. Chop 5 sec / speed 5. Scrape down with spatula. Transfer to a small bowl — label it "Batch 1 carrots."
2
Bowl 2
Add second batch: 180 g carrots and 22 g garlic. Chop 5 sec / speed 5. Scrape down. Transfer to another bowl — label "Batch 2 carrots." Set Bowl 2 aside.
3
Bowl 1
Add 200 g quartered onion. Chop 5 sec / speed 7. Scrape down with spatula.
4
Bowl 1
Add 30 ml olive oil. Sauté 12 min / 120°C / speed 1 until onion is softened and beginning to colour. Measuring cup on lid.
5
By hand — during step 4
Finely chop 36 g total preserved lemon peel (18 g per bowl) by hand. Quarter the second onion (200 g). Measure out spices for both bowls.
6
Bowl 1
Add Batch 1 carrots/garlic and 18 g preserved lemon peel. Sauté 5 min / 120°C / speed 1.
7
Bowl 1
Add 1 tsp Aleppo pepper and 2 tsp ground coriander. Cook 1 min / 120°C / speed 1.
8
Bowl 1
Place simmering basket in bowl with 2 bay leaves and thyme sprigs inside. Add 700 ml vegetable broth, 150 ml water, 200 g rinsed lentils, and plenty of black pepper. Cook 22 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1. Measuring cup on lid.
9
Bowl 2 — swap (~30 sec)
Swap to Bowl 2. Add 200 g quartered second onion. Chop 5 sec / speed 7. Scrape down. Add 30 ml olive oil. Sauté 12 min / 120°C / speed 1. Measuring cup on lid.
10
By hand — during step 9
Herby Oil: finely chop 16 g fresh herbs. Combine with zest of 1 lemon and 30 ml olive oil. Season with flaky sea salt. Adjust to taste — looser is better for spooning.

Yogurt Sauce (optional): stir 240 g yogurt with 20 ml lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
11
Bowl 2
Add Batch 2 carrots/garlic and 18 g preserved lemon peel. Sauté 5 min / 120°C / speed 1.
12
Bowl 2
Add 1 tsp Aleppo pepper and 2 tsp ground coriander. Cook 1 min / 120°C / speed 1.
13
Bowl 2
Place simmering basket with bay leaves and thyme in bowl. Add 700 ml broth, 150 ml water, 200 g rinsed lentils, black pepper. Cook 22 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1. Measuring cup on lid.
14
Bowl 1 — swap when Bowl 2 starts (~30 sec)
Swap to Bowl 1. Lift out simmering basket; discard herbs. Stir in 12 g nutritional yeast and 7 g salt. Cook 2 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1. Remove from heat. Stir in 15 ml lemon juice. Taste and adjust salt and lemon. Transfer to a large pot or serving vessel to free the bowl.
15
Bowl 2 — swap (~30 sec)
Swap to Bowl 2. Lift out simmering basket; discard herbs. Stir in 12 g nutritional yeast and 7 g salt. Cook 2 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1. Remove from heat. Stir in 15 ml lemon juice. Taste and adjust.
16
To serve
Ladle soup into bowls. Spoon herby oil generously over each — don't be shy, this is what makes the soup. Add a dollop of yogurt sauce if using. Serve with crusty bread.

Tips & Tricks

TM6 onion browning: The 120°C cap means you won't get as deep a fond as a screaming-hot Dutch oven. The extended 12-minute sauté compensates well — but if you want maximum caramelization, do the first onion step in a pan, then deglaze into the TM6 bowl before adding carrots.
Preserved lemons: Whole Foods stocks them (Mina brand is reliable in San Dimas area). Chop by hand rather than in the TM6 — the machine over-processes them into mush and you lose the pleasant textural bursts of flavour.
Lentil texture: Reverse speed 1 keeps the lentils intact. For a thicker, more broken-down soup, after cooking remove about a third of the lentils, pulse the bowl 3–4 times at speed 5 with the measuring cup off, then stir the whole lentils back in.
Meal prep storage: Both batches keep 5 days refrigerated. Store herby oil separately — basil blackens by day 2. Hold the lemon juice when freezing and add fresh upon reheating.
