Spiced Vegetable Lentil Soup

Hearty, aromatic, and finished with lemon — a one-bowl Thermomix meal
DeviceTM6
Prep10 min
Cook35 min
Total45 min
Servings6

Ingredients

  • 300 g brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • 200 g onions (~1 large), quartered
  • 150 g carrots (~1 medium), in chunks
  • 60 g celery (~1 stalk), in chunks
  • 15 g garlic (~3 cloves)
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 400 g canned diced tomatoes (fire-roasted preferred)
  • 800 g vegetable stock
  • 5 g cumin, ground
  • 3 g smoked paprika
  • 2 g dried oregano
  • 1 g turmeric
  • 1 g black pepper
  • 80 g fresh kale or spinach, roughly torn
  • 30 ml lemon juice (~1 lemon)
  • 7 g salt (adjust to taste)

Preparation

  1. Place onions, carrots, celery, and garlic into mixing bowl. Chop 5 sec / speed 5. Scrape down sides with spatula.
  2. Add olive oil. Sauté 3 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  3. Add cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, turmeric, and black pepper. Cook 1 min / 120°C / speed 1 until fragrant.
  4. Add diced tomatoes. Cook 2 min / 100°C / speed 1, stirring to concentrate flavors.
  5. Add lentils and vegetable stock. Cook 25 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1. Place measuring cup on lid.
  6. Check lentils for tenderness. If needed, cook 5 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1 more.
  7. Add kale (or spinach), salt, and lemon juice. Cook 3 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1 until greens are wilted.
  8. For a partially creamy texture: ladle approximately 400 g of soup into a separate bowl. Blend remaining soup 10 sec / speed 6. Return reserved portion and stir 10 sec / reverse speed 1.

Tips & Tricks

  • Toasting spices in the hot oil (step 3) before adding liquid unlocks their full aroma — a technique used across Indian, Middle Eastern, and Mexican cuisines.
  • Reverse mode in step 5 is critical: it stirs the lentils without breaking them apart, so you get distinct texture rather than mush.
  • The half-blend in step 8 is optional but highly recommended — it creates a creamy body while keeping chunks. Skip it if you prefer fully rustic or go full blend for a smooth soup.
  • This soup improves overnight. Make extra and store in the fridge for up to 4 days or freeze for up to 3 months.
