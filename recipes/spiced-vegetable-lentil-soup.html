Spiced Vegetable Lentil Soup

Hearty, aromatic, and finished with lemon — a Thermomix one-pot meal
DeviceTM6
Prep10 min
Cook 35 min ~40 min
Total 45 min ~50 min
Servings 6 10–12

Ingredients

  • 300 g brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • 200 g onions (~1 large), quartered
  • 150 g carrots (~1 medium), in chunks
  • 60 g celery (~1 stalk), in chunks
  • 15 g garlic (~3 cloves)
  • 40 g olive oil
  • 400 g canned diced tomatoes (fire-roasted preferred)
  • 800 g vegetable stock
  • 5 g cumin, ground
  • 3 g smoked paprika
  • 2 g dried oregano
  • 1 g turmeric
  • 1 g black pepper
  • 80 g fresh kale or spinach, roughly torn
  • 30 ml lemon juice (~1 lemon)
  • 7 g salt (adjust to taste)

Ingredients (doubled for 2 bowls)

Prep all ingredients, then split evenly between the two runs. Each bowl gets the same recipe at full capacity.

  • 600 g brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • 400 g onions (~2 large), quartered
  • 300 g carrots (~2 medium), in chunks
  • 120 g celery (~2 stalks), in chunks
  • 30 g garlic (~6 cloves)
  • 80 g olive oil
  • 800 g canned diced tomatoes
  • 1600 g vegetable stock
  • 10 g cumin, ground
  • 6 g smoked paprika
  • 4 g dried oregano
  • 2 g turmeric
  • 2 g black pepper
  • 160 g fresh kale or spinach, roughly torn
  • 60 ml lemon juice (~2 lemons)
  • 14 g salt (adjust to taste)

Preparation

  1. Place onions, carrots, celery, and garlic into mixing bowl. Chop 5 sec / speed 5. Scrape down sides with spatula.
  2. Add olive oil. Sauté 3 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  3. Add cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, turmeric, and black pepper. Cook 1 min / 120°C / speed 1 until fragrant.
  4. Add diced tomatoes. Cook 2 min / 100°C / speed 1, stirring to concentrate flavors.
  5. Add lentils and vegetable stock. Cook 25 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1. Place measuring cup on lid.
  6. Check lentils for tenderness. If needed, cook 5 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1 more.
  7. Add kale (or spinach), salt, and lemon juice. Cook 3 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1 until greens are wilted.
  8. For a partially creamy texture: ladle approximately 400 g of soup into a separate bowl. Blend remaining soup 10 sec / speed 6. Return reserved portion and stir 10 sec / reverse speed 1.

Preparation (2 Bowls)

Chop all vegetables in one go using Bowl 1. Start cooking in Bowl 1, then use the simmer time to start Bowl 2.

  1. Bowl 1 Place half the onions, carrots, celery, and garlic into Bowl 1. Chop 5 sec / speed 5. Scrape down. Transfer chopped vegetables to an external bowl and set aside.
  2. Bowl 1 Without cleaning, add the other half of onions, carrots, celery, and garlic. Chop 5 sec / speed 5. Scrape down. Leave in bowl.
  3. Bowl 1 Add 40 g olive oil. Sauté 3 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  4. Bowl 1 Add 5 g cumin, 3 g paprika, 2 g oregano, 1 g turmeric, 1 g pepper. Cook 1 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  5. Bowl 1 Add 400 g diced tomatoes. Cook 2 min / 100°C / speed 1.
  6. Bowl 1 Add 300 g lentils and 800 g stock. Cook 25 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1. Place measuring cup on lid.
  7. Swap While Bowl 1 simmers, place the reserved chopped vegetables into Bowl 2 (off base). Bowl 1 stays cooking.
  8. Bowl 1 When done, add 80 g kale, 7 g salt, 30 ml lemon juice. Cook 3 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1.
  9. Bowl 1 Optional partial blend: ladle 400 g out, blend 10 sec / speed 6, return and stir 10 sec / reverse speed 1. Pour finished soup into serving pot. Remove Bowl 1 from base.
  10. Bowl 2 Place Bowl 2 on base. Add 40 g olive oil. Sauté 3 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  11. Bowl 2 Add 5 g cumin, 3 g paprika, 2 g oregano, 1 g turmeric, 1 g pepper. Cook 1 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  12. Bowl 2 Add 400 g diced tomatoes. Cook 2 min / 100°C / speed 1.
  13. Bowl 2 Add 300 g lentils and 800 g stock. Cook 25 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1. Measuring cup on lid.
  14. Bowl 2 Add 80 g kale, 7 g salt, 30 ml lemon juice. Cook 3 min / 100°C / reverse speed 1.
  15. Bowl 2 Optional partial blend same as step 9. Combine with first batch.

Tips & Tricks

  • Toasting spices in the hot oil before adding liquid unlocks their full aroma — a technique used across Indian, Middle Eastern, and Mexican cuisines.
  • Reverse mode is critical during simmering: it stirs the lentils without breaking them apart, giving you distinct texture rather than mush.
  • The half-blend at the end is optional but recommended — it creates a creamy body while keeping chunks. Skip for rustic, go full blend for smooth.
  • This soup improves overnight. Store in the fridge up to 4 days or freeze up to 3 months.
  • 2-bowl tip: Total wall-clock time is ~50 min for double the yield. You use Bowl 1's 25-min simmer as downtime — Bowl 2 is already prepped and waiting.
